# Sound Academy Curriculum Governance

## Version Control Strategy

- Curriculum architecture documents are stored in Git under `docs/phase-0`.
- Seed content changes require pull requests.
- Production curriculum records must include version, status and owner.
- Major changes to X32 routing, Dante topology, Logic templates or certification rules require Technical Director approval.

## Review Cycles

- Weekly: review service incidents and convert repeat issues into lessons, SOP updates or Sound Lab scenarios.
- Monthly: review certification progress, mentor backlog and Sound Lab failure patterns.
- Quarterly: review academy structure, equipment inventory, service-mode checklists and renewal rules.
- Annually: renew certification standards and archive outdated equipment workflows.

## Approval Workflows

Draft:

- Content can be edited but not assigned.

Review:

- Senior Engineer checks technical accuracy.
- Learning owner checks clarity and progression.

Approved:

- Technical Director or Admin publishes.

Retired:

- Content remains archived but is hidden from normal learner paths.

## Content Ownership

- Foundations: Senior Engineer and Training Lead
- FOH Operator: Senior Engineer
- Livestream: Livestream Lead
- Dante/Systems: Technical Director or Systems Engineer
- SOPs: Technical Director
- Equipment: Admin plus equipment owner
- Certification rules: Technical Director

## Quality Standards

Every module must be:

- Church-specific
- Service-relevant
- Practical
- Assessable
- Linked to equipment/SOP/troubleshooting where relevant
- Written in plain language for volunteers
- Free of lorem ipsum

Every practical lesson must include:

- What to do
- Where to do it
- What to observe
- Why it matters
- What can go wrong
- How a mentor signs it off

## Retirement Strategy

Retire or revise content when:

- Equipment changes
- X32 showfile/routing changes
- Dante naming or topology changes
- Logic template changes
- Waves chain changes
- SOP no longer matches Sunday practice
- Incident review shows the lesson causes confusion

## Asset Governance

Asset states:

- Needed
- Captured
- Reviewed
- Approved
- Rejected
- Retired

Church-owned captures are required for:

- Actual X32 layout and showfile states
- Dante device names and routing matrix
- Logic template, channel strips and buses
- OBS/encoder handoff
- Wireless storage/prep workflow
- Choir mic placement
- Stage monitor and P16 setup

Vendor references are allowed for:

- General product orientation
- Official UI/documentation references
- Plugin feature explanations

## Hard Rule

Do not mass-generate lesson content until:

- Academy structure is approved
- Certifications are defined
- Competencies are mapped
- Assessment architecture exists
- Progression rules are documented
- Mentor framework is ready
