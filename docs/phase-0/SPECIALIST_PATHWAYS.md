# Sound Academy Specialist Pathways

## Governing Signal Chain

All specialist pathways map back to the real ministry signal chain:

`Source -> Mic/DI/Wireless -> Stagebox/Local Input -> X32 -> FOH / Monitors / P16 / Dante -> Logic Pro -> Waves Processing -> OBS/Encoder -> Stream`

The trainee is not certified because they watched lessons. They are certified when they can safely operate the actual church signal chain, diagnose faults under pressure, explain decisions and receive mentor sign-off during real services.

## X32 FOH Specialist

Competencies:

- Navigate selected channel, Home, Config, Gate, Dynamics, EQ, Sends, Routing, Scenes and meters.
- Set safe preamp gain using realistic speech/singing level.
- Distinguish gain, trim, fader, DCA and bus-send level.
- Build a clean vocal channel with HPF, corrective EQ, light compression and routing.
- Use mute groups, DCAs, scenes and snippets safely.
- Route sources to P16, monitor buses, matrices and livestream buses.

Practical assessments:

- Set gain on pastor mic and sung vocal without clipping.
- Reduce feedback risk without destroying tone.
- Route a vocal to FOH, livestream bus and P16.
- Recall the correct service scene and explain what changed.
- Recover from “channel has meter but no room sound.”

## Dante Specialist

Competencies:

- Identify Dante devices, transmitters, receivers and subscriptions.
- Check network interface, sample rate, clock leader, latency and device visibility.
- Understand Dante Virtual Soundcard as the DAW bridge.
- Trace X32/card output to Logic input.
- Recover broken subscriptions calmly.

Practical assessments:

- Verify X32-to-Logic routing in Dante Controller.
- Diagnose “Logic receives no signal.”
- Fix a missing Dante subscription.
- Explain clock sync and sample-rate mismatch symptoms.
- Complete pre-service Dante health check in under three minutes.

## Logic Livestream Specialist

Competencies:

- Explain FOH versus livestream mix goals.
- Open approved church Logic template.
- Confirm audio device, Dante inputs, track names, auxes, buses and outputs.
- Build speech, vocal, choir, worship, audience mic and stream master paths.
- Use loudness metering, limiting and translation checks.
- Manage latency and plugin delay safely.

Practical assessments:

- Start Logic from cold and verify live signal from X32/Dante.
- Build or validate a vocal path into stream master.
- Correct a stream that is too quiet, too dry, too roomy or clipping.
- Check translation on phone, headphones, laptop and TV-style speakers.
- Handoff clean audio to OBS or stream encoder.

## Waves Plugin Specialist

Teach by purpose, not plugin name.

Competencies:

- EQ: remove mud, harshness and low rumble.
- Dynamic EQ: tame harsh vocal ranges without dulling the whole vocal.
- Compression: control level while preserving worship dynamics.
- Multiband compression: manage problem ranges on stream bus or vocal bus.
- De-essing: reduce vocal bite and speech sibilance.
- Limiting: protect stream output without pumping.
- Metering: interpret loudness and true peak.

Practical assessments:

- Apply Waves F6 to harsh vocal around the problem band.
- Use C6 or similar only when static EQ is not enough.
- Use Renaissance Compressor on speech or vocal with musical gain reduction.
- Compare good compression versus over-compression in Sound Lab.
- Build approved speech, vocal, choir, worship bus and livestream master chains.

## P16 and Monitor Specialist

Competencies:

- Explain ULTRANET/P16 source order.
- Confirm each P16 channel source.
- Set musician-friendly gain and balance without destabilising FOH.
- Know when to adjust P16 source send versus musician local P16 knob.
- Understand talkback routing.

Practical assessments:

- Help a vocalist hear themselves in P16.
- Diagnose “P16 user cannot hear keys.”
- Confirm all P16 sources before rehearsal.
- Route talkback without sending it to livestream.

## Wireless and Choir Specialist

Competencies:

- Check batteries, mute state, RF status, capsule/pack condition and receiver output.
- Label pastor, worship leader and handheld mics correctly.
- Understand choir mic placement, pickup pattern and feedback risk.
- Use HPF and gentle EQ for choir clarity.
- Manage stage volume and mic distance.

Practical assessments:

- Prepare wireless mics before service.
- Fix “pastor mic distorted.”
- Fix “wireless cutting out.”
- Position choir mics for usable level before feedback.
- Reduce choir feedback without muting the choir.

## FOH and Livestream Joint Pathway

FOH and livestream are related but not identical jobs.

FOH competencies:

- Speech intelligibility in the room.
- Worship balance in the PA.
- Feedback prevention.
- Stage volume awareness.
- Monitor impact on FOH.

Livestream competencies:

- Vocal and speech clarity on small speakers.
- Consistent loudness.
- Audience mic balance.
- Stereo image and bus control.
- Plugin latency and stream safety.

Joint service drill:

1. One trainee mixes FOH while another runs Logic stream.
2. Mentor introduces a fault: muted bus, low Dante input, harsh vocal, wireless battery warning or feedback buildup.
3. Trainees communicate, identify where the signal is still healthy, fix the issue and log the incident.

## Required Troubleshooting Scenarios

- No sound from mic
- Meter on X32 but no room sound
- Room has sound but stream has no sound
- Logic receives no signal
- Dante device missing
- Dante subscription broken
- Wireless mic cutting out
- Pastor mic distorted
- Choir mics feeding back
- P16 user cannot hear themselves
- Talkback not working
- X32 scene recall wrong
- Vocal too harsh
- Mix too muddy
- Stream too quiet
- Stream clipping
- Latency or plugin delay causing stream issues

Each scenario must define:

- First safe check
- What to observe
- What it means
- Where to look next
- What not to touch during service
- Escalation point
- Related SOP
- Related lesson
- Related equipment
- Incident log template

## Church-Specific Versus Generic

Church-specific required:

- Actual X32 showfiles, scenes, snippets and routing
- Exact Dante device names and subscriptions
- Actual Logic livestream template
- Approved Waves chains
- P16 source order
- Wireless mic labels, pack assignments and battery process
- Choir mic positions
- Talkback routing
- OBS/encoder handoff
- Service checklist and escalation contacts
- Photos/screenshots of booth, console, stagebox, Dante Controller, Logic template and wireless rack

Generic allowed:

- Meaning of gain, EQ, compression, gating and routing
- General X32 navigation concepts
- General Dante concepts
- General Logic bus/aux concepts
- General Waves plugin purposes
- General feedback, intelligibility and gain-before-feedback principles
